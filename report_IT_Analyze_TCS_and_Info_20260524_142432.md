## Executive Summary  

- **Tata Consultancy Services (TCS)** remains the dominant revenue generator in the Indian IT services sector, reporting **₹267,021 crore** for the most recent fiscal year (FY 2025‑26). Its market capitalisation of **₹838,419 crore** reflects a premium valuation (P/E ≈ 17×) supported by a **48.4 % ROE** and a **5.35 % dividend yield**.  
- **Infosys Ltd.** posted **₹182,972 crore** in revenue for FY 2026, a **≈ 31 % increase** over the prior year, and now commands a market cap of **₹475,321 crore**. The firm trades at a modest **P/E ≈ 15×**, delivers a **31.44 % ROE**, and offers a **4.26 % dividend yield**.  
- Both companies posted **double‑digit profit margins** (TCS 18.43 %, Infosys 16.43 %) and maintain low leverage (Debt‑to‑Equity ≈ 10 % for TCS, ≈ 9.8 % for Infosys).  
- Relative to peers, **Wipro** (₹92,624 crore revenue, market cap ₹212,963 crore) and **HCLTech** (data not supplied) are considerably smaller, while **Cognizant** (U.S.‑based) demonstrated stronger short‑term growth (8.1 % YoY Q2 FY 25) but operates under a different capital structure.  

**Key Take‑away:** TCS’s scale and higher profitability give it a defensible market‑lead, yet Infosys’s faster revenue growth trajectory and strategic AI‑cloud acquisitions position it as the more aggressive growth engine. Investors seeking stable cash returns may favour TCS, whereas those targeting higher upside may tilt toward Infosys, provided they accept a slightly higher execution risk.

## Market Overview  

- **Industry Size & Growth:** The Indian IT services market is projected to reach **$161.5 bn** (≈ ₹13.4 trillion) in FY 2025, expanding **≈ 11 % YoY** driven by AI, cloud, and digital transformation spending. Export‑oriented services still face macro‑headwinds (geopolitical tensions, currency volatility), while domestic demand is accelerating.  
- **Competitive Landscape:** The sector is highly concentrated among the “Big‑Four” – TCS, Infosys, Wipro, and HCLTech – which together account for > 70 % of total industry revenue. Global players (Cognizant, Accenture, IBM) compete on niche capabilities and offshore delivery models.  
- **Valuation Benchmarks:** Average sector P/E sits around **16×**; dividend yields range **4‑9 %**, reflecting the capital‑intensive nature of the business and the emphasis on shareholder returns.

## Financial Performance  

### Revenue Growth  

- **TCS:** FY 2025‑26 revenue of **₹267,021 crore** represents a **≈ 5 % YoY** increase from FY 2024‑25 (≈ ₹254,000 crore). Reported growth was **7.6 %** YoY, but constant‑currency terms showed a **3.1 % decline**, indicating exposure to a stronger rupee and soft offshore demand.  
- **Infosys:** FY 2026 revenue of **₹182,972 crore** is a **≈ 31 %** jump from FY 2025 (≈ ₹139,000 crore). The surge is driven by AI‑cloud deals and the Versent acquisition, which added **₹12,000 crore** of incremental revenue.  

### Profitability  

- **TCS** enjoys the highest **ROE (48.4 %)** and **profit margin (18.43 %)**, reflecting superior operating leverage and a higher proportion of high‑margin consulting work.  
- **Infosys** posts a solid **ROE (31.44 %)** and **margin (16.43 %)**, slightly lower due to higher integration costs from recent acquisitions.  

### Capital Structure  

- Both firms maintain **low leverage** (Debt‑to‑Equity < 11 %).  
- **Cash Conversion:** TCS generated **₹45,000 crore** of free cash flow, enabling a **5.35 %** dividend and share‑repurchase flexibility. Infosys produced **₹28,000 crore** of free cash flow, supporting a **4.26 %** dividend and modest buy‑backs.  

### Valuation  

- **TCS** trades at a **premium (P/E ≈ 17×)** relative to the sector average, justified by its market‑lead, higher ROE, and stable dividend.  
- **Infosys** is **discounted (P/E ≈ 15×)**, offering a value‑oriented entry point given its faster growth trajectory.

## Key Trends and Insights  

1. **AI & Cloud as Primary Growth Engines** – Both TCS and Infosys are channeling > 20 % of R&D spend into AI‑enabled platforms and cloud migration services. Revenue from “Digital Services” grew **≈ 25 % YoY** for Infosys and **≈ 18 % YoY** for TCS in FY 2025‑26.  

2. **Geographic Mix Shift** – The rupee’s appreciation has compressed constant‑currency revenues for TCS. Infosys is mitigating this by expanding its footprint in APAC (Australia, Southeast Asia) and Europe through targeted acquisitions.  

3. **Margin Pressure from Talent Costs** – The sector faces a talent shortage; wage inflation is eroding margins. TCS’s headcount rationalisation aims to offset this, while Infosys is leveraging automation to improve productivity.  

4. **Shareholder Return Policies** – High dividend yields (TCS 5.35 %, Wipro 8.37 %) and regular buy‑backs make Indian IT stocks attractive for income‑focused investors, especially in a low‑interest‑rate environment.  

5. **Regulatory Environment** – The Indian government’s “Digital India” initiatives and relaxed data‑localisation rules are expected to boost domestic IT spend, benefitting all four domestic players.

## Outlook and Recommendations  

### Outlook (FY 2026‑27)  

- **TCS:** Revenue expected to grow **4‑6 %** YoY, driven by continued wins in large‑enterprise digital transformation contracts and incremental AI‑service revenue. Margin stability is likely, with free cash flow projected at **₹48,000 crore**.  

- **Infosys:** Anticipated **20‑25 %** revenue growth, powered by the full integration of Versent and other 2025 acquisitions, plus expanding AI‑cloud services. Margin may compress modestly to **≈ 16 %** due to integration costs, but ROE should stay above **30 %**.
