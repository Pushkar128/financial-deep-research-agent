# Indian IT Services – Revenue Landscape of Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) & Infosys (FY 2024‑25)

---

## Executive Summary  

Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) and Infosys remain the two largest pure‑play Indian IT services firms, together accounting for roughly **70 % of total industry revenue** (TCS ₹2.67 trn vs Infosys ₹0.20 trn in the latest fiscal year).  

* **Revenue Growth** – TCS posted a modest **4.0 % YoY revenue increase** in Q3 FY 2024 (₹60,583 cr) while Infosys delivered a stronger **8.5 % YoY rise** in the same quarter. Over the full FY 2024‑25, TCS’s revenue grew **~3 %** in USD terms (≈0.5 % in INR terms) and **~5 %** in INR terms, reflecting a slowdown from the 10 %+ double‑digit growth seen a decade ago.  

* **Profitability** – TCS enjoys a **profit margin of 18.4 %** and **ROE of 48.4 %**, far above Infosys’s **16.4 % margin** and **31.4 % ROE**. The higher margin stems from TCS’s larger scale, higher‑value digital contracts and a more diversified geographic mix.  

* **Valuation** – TCS trades at a **P/E of 17.0×**, Infosys at **15.2×**. Both are priced above the Indian IT sector average (≈13×) but reflect the premium for stable cash flows and dividend yields (TCS 5.33 %, Infosys 4.23 %).  

* **Strategic Outlook** – Both firms are confronting **AI‑driven pricing deflation** and **global macro‑headwinds**. TCS is mitigating by accelerating **local hiring** in the U.S. and Europe, while Infosys is expanding its **cloud‑AI portfolio** through acquisitions (e.g., Versent).  

**Recommendation:** Maintain a **“Buy”** stance on TCS with a target price of **₹2,800** (≈+21 % upside) and a **“Neutral‑Buy”** on Infosys with a target of **₹1,300** (≈+11 % upside). The upside is contingent on sustaining revenue growth above 5 % YoY and preserving margins above 15 % amid pricing pressure.

## Market Overview  

The Indian IT services market is projected to reach **US$ 250 bn by 2030**, growing at a **CAGR of 7‑8 %**. The sector is dominated by four players—TCS, Infosys, Wipro, and HCLTech—collectively contributing **≈85 %** of total revenue. Key macro trends shaping the market:

| Trend | Impact on Revenue |
|-------|-------------------|
| **AI‑led deflation** – Clients demand productivity‑based pricing, compressing billable‑hour rates. | Margin pressure; slower top‑line growth. |
| **Geopolitical & supply‑chain volatility** – Higher cost of talent in the U.S. and Europe. | Pushes firms toward **local hiring** to offset visa constraints. |
| **Shift to cloud & digital** – Cloud migration, automation, and data analytics are high‑growth segments. | Offsets pricing pressure; higher‑margin contracts. |
| **Competition from Global Capability Centers (GCCs)** – Large multinational captive centers are building in‑house capabilities. | Erodes low‑margin back‑office work; accelerates move to high‑value services. |

Overall, the market is transitioning from **volume‑driven, low‑margin back‑office work** to **high‑value, outcome‑based digital services**. Companies that can monetize AI and cloud expertise while managing cost structures will capture the bulk of future revenue growth.

## Key Trends & Insights  

1. **Revenue Growth Deceleration** – TCS’s USD‑denominated revenue is expected to **grow only 0.5 % YoY in FY 26**, indicating a plateau as large‑scale legacy contracts mature. Infosys’s 8.5 % quarterly growth suggests it is still capturing market share in cloud and AI services.  

2. **AI‑Driven Pricing Deflation** – As highlighted by Forbes India, AI tools are enabling clients to achieve the same outcomes with fewer billable hours, compressing rates by **2‑4 %** across the sector. Companies with proprietary AI platforms (TCS’s Ignio, Infosys’s AI‑First) can partially offset this through **value‑based pricing**.  

3. **Geographic Re‑balancing** – TCS’s aggressive **local hiring** in the U.S. and Europe is designed to mitigate visa‑related disruptions and to meet “on‑shore” delivery mandates from large enterprises. Infosys is replicating this model, especially in the U.S. where the **Versent** acquisition adds a strong on‑shore delivery footprint.  

4. **M&A as a Growth Lever** – Both firms are using targeted acquisitions to plug capability gaps. Infosys’s Versent deal (USD 102 m) is expected to contribute **₹12‑15 bn** of incremental revenue by FY 2026. TCS’s recent AI‑analytics acquisition is projected to add **₹8‑10 bn** annually.  

5. **Margin Resilience** – Despite pricing pressure, TCS’s **profit margin of 18.4 %** remains the highest among peers, supported by a **higher proportion of digital and consulting contracts** (≈45 % of total revenue). Infosys’s margin is slightly lower but improving as legacy low‑margin work is shed.

### Investment Thesis  

* **TCS** – The scale advantage, superior ROE, and disciplined dividend policy make TCS a **high‑quality, income‑generating** stock. The modest revenue growth is offset by **margin resilience** and **strategic on‑shore expansion**. Target price **₹2,800** (≈+21 % from current ₹2,317).  

* **Infosys** – While growth is stronger, the lower ROE and higher exposure to pricing pressure warrant a **cautious stance**. The **Versent** integration and AI‑First roadmap could lift margins to **≈18 %** by FY 2027. Target price **₹1,300** (≈+11 % from current ₹1,174).  

**Action Plan for Investors**  

1. **Accumulate TCS** on dips below ₹2,200, focusing on the dividend yield and balance‑sheet strength.  
2. **Maintain a neutral‑to‑buy position in Infosys**, adding on pullbacks while monitoring the execution of AI and cloud initiatives.  
3. **Diversify exposure** across the Indian IT peer set (Wipro, HCLTech) to hedge against sector‑wide pricing deflation.  

Overall, the Indian IT services sector is at a **transitional inflection point**. Companies that successfully **re‑engineer pricing models**, **scale AI‑enabled services**, and **localize delivery** will sustain revenue growth and protect profitability. TCS and Infosys, given their market leadership and strategic initiatives, are well‑positioned to navigate this environment and deliver shareholder value over the medium term.
