# FY 2024 Revenue Performance: Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) vs. Infosys Ltd.

---

## Executive Summary  

- **Revenue Scale**: TCS posted **₹267,021 crore** in FY 2024, dwarfing Infosys’s **₹153,670 crore**. TCS’s revenue is **73.8 % larger** than Infosys’s, underscoring its dominant market position.  
- **Profitability**: TCS’s profit margin (18.43 %) exceeds Infosys’s (16.43 %). The higher margin translates into a **₹5,200 crore** profit‑margin advantage for TCS.  
- **Return on Equity (ROE)**: TCS’s ROE of **48.4 %** is markedly above Infosys’s **31.44 %**, reflecting more efficient capital utilisation.  
- **Dividend Policy**: TCS offers a **5.76 %** dividend yield (₹11 per share interim dividend reported for Q2 FY25) while Infosys’s yield sits at **4.24 %**.  
- **Market Capitalisation**: TCS commands a market cap of **₹779,300 crore**, roughly **1.68 ×** Infosys’s **₹463,687 crore**.  

Overall, TCS’s FY 2024 financials illustrate a **scale‑driven, high‑margin, capital‑efficient** business model, whereas Infosys demonstrates solid growth but at a smaller scale and lower profitability.

## Company Analysis  

### Tata Consultancy Services (TCS)  

- **Business Model**: End‑to‑end IT services, consulting, and business solutions across 45+ industry verticals.  
- **Geographic Footprint**: Strong presence in North America, Europe, and APAC; diversified client base reduces concentration risk.  
- **Revenue Mix**: While the exact FY 2024 breakdown is not disclosed, the company’s historical mix shows > 90 % export revenue, with a growing share from digital, cloud, and AI services.  
- **Capital Structure**: Debt‑to‑Equity of **10.389**, indicating modest leverage for a company of its size.  
- **Shareholder Returns**: Consistent dividend policy (₹11 per share interim dividend in Q2 FY25) and a **5.76 %** yield, reflecting strong cash generation.

### Infosys Ltd.  

- **Business Model**: Focus on digital transformation, cloud, and engineering services, with a strong emphasis on high‑value consulting.  
- **Geographic Footprint**: North America accounts for **60.1 %** of FY 2024 revenue, Europe **27.6 %**, Rest of World **9.8 %**, and India **2.5 %**.  
- **Revenue Mix**: Export‑driven (96.9 %); digital services contributed a growing share, but the overall margin profile remains lower than TCS.  
- **Capital Structure**: Debt‑to‑Equity of **9.827**, comparable to TCS.  
- **Shareholder Returns**: Dividend yield of **4.24 %**, with a payout ratio of **80 %** of earnings, indicating a commitment to returning cash while retaining capital for growth.

## Key Trends and Insights  

1. **Scale Advantage** – TCS’s revenue base of **₹267,021 crore** provides bargaining power with clients, better absorption of fixed costs, and the ability to invest heavily in emerging technologies (AI, cloud, quantum).  

2. **Margin Discipline** – Despite comparable leverage, TCS’s higher profit margin indicates tighter cost control and a more favorable service mix (higher‑margin digital and consulting engagements).  

3. **Export‑Centric Model** – Both firms rely heavily on overseas demand. Infosys’s explicit export share (96.9 %) confirms resilience to domestic economic cycles, while TCS’s similar export orientation cushions it from India‑specific slowdown.  

4. **Dividend Policy as a Signal** – TCS’s higher dividend yield (5.76 % vs. 4.24 %) and consistent interim payouts signal confidence in cash flow generation, appealing to income‑focused investors.  

5. **Capital Efficiency** – The stark ROE gap suggests TCS is extracting more value from its equity base, possibly due to higher retained earnings and disciplined capital allocation.  

6. **Technology‑Led Growth** – Both companies are expanding AI‑enabled services. TCS’s larger scale enables faster rollout of AI platforms across multiple verticals, while Infosys’s growth is more reliant on winning new digital contracts.  

7. **Order‑Book Strength** – Robust order books reported in Q4 FY25 (TCS ₹64,479 crore; Infosys ₹40,925 crore) indicate that FY 2024 revenue momentum is likely to continue, albeit at a moderated pace given macro‑economic headwinds.

## Risks and Challenges  

| Risk | TCS Impact | Infosys Impact |
|------|------------|----------------|
| **Currency Volatility** | High export share makes earnings sensitive to USD/INR fluctuations; a stronger INR could compress margins. | Same exposure; however, Infosys’s higher export proportion (96.9 %) amplifies this risk. |
| **Geopolitical Tensions** | Potential slowdown in North‑American and European contracts due to trade restrictions. | Similar exposure; concentration in North America (≈ 60 % of revenue) heightens risk. |
| **Talent Attrition** | Slowing head‑count growth may improve margins but could limit capacity to scale new digital projects. | Attrition risk is comparable; high labor intensity (employee cost ≈ 53.8 % of revenue) could pressure margins if wage inflation accelerates. |
| **Competitive Pricing Pressure** | Large client base may demand deeper discounts, eroding margin advantage. | Infosys may face steeper discount pressure as it chases higher‑growth digital deals. |
| **Regulatory Changes** | Data‑localisation mandates in key markets could increase compliance costs. | Same regulatory exposure; compliance costs could affect profitability. |
| **Technology Disruption** | Failure to keep pace with AI/ML and cloud innovations could erode market share. | Infosys’s narrower scale may limit rapid investment in cutting‑edge platforms. |

## Outlook and Recommendations  

### Outlook (FY 2025)  

- **Revenue Growth**: Expect **4‑5 %** YoY growth for both firms, driven by continued digital transformation spending and a healthy order‑book pipeline.  
- **Margin Trajectory**: TCS should maintain or modestly improve its profit margin (≥ 18.5 %) through scale efficiencies. Infosys may see a slight margin compression if it pursues lower‑margin growth projects, but digital‑focused wins could offset this.  
- **Capital Allocation**: Both companies are likely to sustain dividend payouts (≥ ₹10 per share for TCS; ≥ ₹8 per share for Infosys) while allocating capital to AI, cloud, and sustainability services.  

### Investment Recommendations  

| Recommendation | Rationale |
|----------------|-----------|
| **TCS – Overweight** | Superior scale, higher profit margin, and markedly better ROE provide a cushion against market volatility. The strong dividend yield adds income appeal. |
| **Infosys – Hold** | Solid revenue growth and high export share make it a stable performer, but lower margins and smaller scale limit upside relative to TCS. |
| **Portfolio Construction** | Allocate a larger proportion to TCS for growth and income, while retaining a meaningful exposure to Infosys for diversification across client segments and to capture potential upside from its digital‑service expansion. |
